Know more about Indian Coal Mines

Largest Coal Producer

India is the second-largest coal producer globally, after China. In 2023, it produced over 850 million metric tons of coal.

Major Coal Reserves

India's coal reserves are primarily found in the eastern and central states, with Jharkhand, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, West Bengal, and Madhya Pradesh as key producers.

Types of Coal

The majority of India's coal is thermal coal used for electricity generation, with smaller quantities of metallurgical coal for steel production.

Coal India Limited (CIL)

Coal India Limited is the largest coal-producing company in the world, contributing around 80% of India's domestic coal production.

Imports and Exports

India imports coal, especially coking coal for steel production. Australia and Indonesia are key suppliers, and exports are minimal.

Mining Methods

India mainly uses open-cast mining, with underground mining contributing less to total production.

Electricity Generation

Coal powers around 70% of India's electricity generation, with demand increasing due to energy needs.

Environmental Impact

Coal mining and burning contribute to air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions, prompting discussions on transitioning to renewable energy.

Monetization of Coal Blocks

Since 2020, India has auctioned coal blocks to private companies, transitioning from public sector dominance.

Employment

The coal industry provides jobs for millions of workers in mining operations, logistics, and power generation.
